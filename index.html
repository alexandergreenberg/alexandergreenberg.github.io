I am a PhD student in Philosophy at the University of Cambridge. Before my PhD, I studied for an MPhil in Philosophy at Cambridge, and a BA in Philosophy and Ancient Greek at University College London.

My research centres on psychological, epistemological, and normative questions about belief, and how these questions interrelate. These questions include:

  • What distinguishes believing from other attitudes like imagining, supposing, or guessing?
  • How do we have knowledge of our own beliefs? Is it through conscious awareness, inference, or some other means?
  • What makes a belief justified, rational, or knowledge?
  • What kind of norms govern belief? How do they differ from (or similar to) practical and moral norms governing action?
  • In what sense, if any, do we count as responsible for our beliefs? Does this responsibility require direct voluntary control over our beliefs?

My PhD thesis is on epistemic normativity. In particular, it argues that belief is subject to a truth norm that prescribes that one ought to believe something if and only if it's true. I argue for this on the grounds that belief being subject to such a norm can give a unifying explanation of epistemic justification. My supervisors are Tim Crane and Arif Ahmed.
